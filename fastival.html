Makar Sankranti

Makar Sankranti is one of the most important festivals in India, celebrated with great joy and enthusiasm. It is also known as the Harvest Festival and is celebrated every year on January 14th. It is believed that the Sun moves into the zodiac sign of Capricorn (Makar). Makar Sankranti brings people together to celebrate the beginning of the harvest season, the triumph of light over darkness and the significance of the new beginning.

DIWALI

Diwali, one of the major religious festivals in Hinduism, Jainism, and Sikhism, lasting for five days from the 13th day of the dark half of the lunar month Ashvina to the second day of the light half of the lunar month Karttika. (The corresponding dates in the Gregorian calendar usually fall in late October and November.) The name is derived from the Sanskrit term dipavali, meaning “row of lights.” The festival generally symbolizes the victory of light over darkness.

Ugadi, also known as Yugadi, is a Hindu festival marking the start of the new year according to the lunisolar calendar in the Deccan region of India. It is celebrated in Andhra Pradesh, Telangana, Karnataka, and Maharashtra (as Gudi Padwa). The festival falls in late March or early April and is a time for new beginnings, celebrated with traditional rituals, delicious food, and cultural festivities.

Sirimanothsavam

The annual Sirimanotsavam festival at the Pydithalli Ammavari temple in Vizianagaram is a major event, typically held in September or October, following Vijayadasami. It's a celebration honoring Goddess Pydimamba, the presiding deity of the temple.
